Steve Jobs

1955–2011

His vision for personal computing and digital media revolutionized industries and continues to influence the world today.

Early Life and Education

Born in San Francisco in 1955, Steve Jobs was adopted and raised in Silicon Valley. He dropped out of Reed College, but his time there influenced his love for typography and design, shaping Apple's future products.

Founding Apple

In 1976, Steve Jobs co-founded Apple with Steve Wozniak in his parents' garage. The company introduced the Apple I and later the Macintosh, revolutionizing personal computing with graphical user interfaces and design-driven products.

NeXT and Pixar

After leaving Apple in 1985, Jobs founded NeXT and acquired Pixar, which became the most successful animation studio in the world. Pixar's movies, such as Toy Story, were critical and commercial successes.

Return to Apple and the iRevolution

Jobs returned to Apple in 1997 and transformed the company with the introduction of the iMac, iPod, iPhone, and iPad. These products redefined industries, from computers to music and telecommunications, making Apple one of the world's most valuable companies.

"Innovation distinguishes between a leader and a follower." – Steve Jobs

Legacy

Steve Jobs passed away in 2011, but his legacy continues to influence the technology industry. His focus on design, simplicity, and user experience remains a cornerstone of modern innovation. Today, Apple products are synonymous with quality and creativity.
